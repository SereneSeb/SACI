### Summary  
Lammas is the UKâ€™s first authorized low-impact ecovillage, established under the One Planet Development policy. Residents live in self-built natural homes, practice permaculture, and govern land as a living commons.

---

### Design Strategy  
- Natural buildings designed and constructed by residents  
- Use of local timber, straw, turf, clay, and recycled materials  
- Passive solar orientation, compost toilets, rainwater harvesting  
- Each home integrates food-growing, energy generation, and water cycling

---

### Ecological Function  
- Energy self-sufficient through solar and micro-hydro systems  
- Permaculture zones and food forests support biodiversity  
- Dry sanitation, reed beds, and greywater reuse systems  
- Minimal external input and near-zero carbon footprint

---

### Community Role  
- Residents form a cooperative and uphold shared land governance  
- Decision-making is horizontal, iterative, and transparent  
- Visitors, apprenticeships, and public events expand educational impact  
- Income tied to land-based productivity, not speculation

---
