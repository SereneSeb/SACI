### Summary  
Breathe is a modular housing prototype that offers compact, flexible, and sustainable urban living for mobile individuals in dense city environments.

---

### Design Strategy  
- Modular timber structure adaptable to small urban plots  
- Lightweight membranes and open partitioning  
- Passive daylighting and ventilation strategies  
- Compact design focused on simplicity and livability

---

### Ecological Function  
- Prefabrication minimizes construction waste  
- Recycled materials reduce embodied carbon  
- Designed for temporary, reversible urban use  
- Encourages efficient use of underutilized urban land

---

### Community Role  
- Prototype challenges norms of homeownership and permanence  
- Encourages cooperative use of unused or interim land  
- Can support seasonal, transient, or precarious urban dwellers  
- Suggests future potential for decentralized urban living networks

---

### Cultural / Symbolic Layer  
- A critique of fixed, consumption-heavy lifestyles  
- Elevates mobility and adaptation as architectural values  
- Asks: What kind of shelter do we really need in the city?  
- Architecture as social provocation and spatial experiment

---

### Healing Design Principles  
- Open, breathable spaces enhance sensory comfort  
- Natural textures and daylight contribute to emotional well-being  
- Scaled to human rhythm and soft material presence  
- Autonomy through spatial clarity and reduction

---

### Outcomes & Impact  
- Generated international attention as a housing provocation  
- Highlighted lightweight, ecological, and flexible construction methods  
- Inspired academic and civic debates around flexible urbanism  
- Informed broader discourse on micro-living and nomadic shelter

---

### Lessons & Transferability  
- Urban space can accommodate flexibility and mobility  
- Small-footprint housing can offer dignity and beauty  
- Modularity and prefabrication suit interim urban needs  
- Spatial minimalism can respond to both climate and lifestyle shifts
