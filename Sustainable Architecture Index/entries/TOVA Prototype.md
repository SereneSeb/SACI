### Summary  
TOVA is a 3D printed earth-based prototype developed to explore climate-adaptive, affordable housing for rural and semi-arid environments.

---

### Design Strategy  
- Single-room dome form for climate responsiveness  
- Printed on-site using local materials  
- No foundation, steel, or synthetic insulation required  
- Renewable energy powers the fabrication process

---

### Ecological Function  
- Minimizes transportation and embodied energy  
- Breathable walls regulate temperature and humidity  
- Fully compostable or recyclable into soil  
- Design aligns with semi-arid, low-resource conditions

---

### Community Role  
- Supports autonomous, off-grid habitation  
- Adaptable to informal and low-infrastructure contexts  
- Offers dignity in places often served by tents or concrete blocks  
- Empowers local construction with minimal tooling

---

### Healing Design Principles  
- Enclosure promotes sensory grounding and thermal comfort  
- Off-grid autonomy reduces precarity and stress  
- Curved space promotes a calming spatial experience  
- Aligns the inhabitant with the pace of the environment
