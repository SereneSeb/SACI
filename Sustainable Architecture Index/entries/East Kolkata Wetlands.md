### Summary  
An ancient water commons that turns sewage into sustenanceâ€”East Kolkata Wetlands transform urban effluent into fertile ecosystems through human-nature reciprocity and informal stewardship.

---

### Design Strategy  
- Decentralized water filtration using pond systems and aquatic ecology  
- Organic infrastructure: clay berms, bamboo fencing, woven walkways  
- Water flow regulated by gravity, tide, and handmade sluices  
- Built through adaptation, not blueprint

---

### Ecological Function  
- Filters over 900 million liters of wastewater daily  
- Produces vegetables and fish for millions  
- Sequesters carbon, stabilizes microclimate, and maintains biodiversity  
- Prevents urban flooding and reduces treatment infrastructure needs

---

### Community Role  
- 50,000+ residents live and work within the wetland matrix  
- Livelihoods tied to stewardship: fishing, farming, foraging  
- Informal institutions self-organize maintenance and seasonal shifts  
- Transgenerational knowledge preserves ecological tuning

---

### Cultural / Symbolic Layer

- Water transformed through patience and patterned flow  
- Landscape as prayer: intimate, improvisational, unfinished  
- Ecological time replaces mechanical time
