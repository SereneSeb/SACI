### Summary  
A pioneering timber housing project that redefines sustainability, material culture, and public-private cooperation in dense Nordic cities.

---

### Design Strategy  
- Structural and envelope systems built entirely in mass timber  
- Emphasis on breathable, biophilic interiors with passive performance  
- Highly energy-efficient glazing and rooftop plantings  
- Modular planning and rapid on-site assembly with minimal waste  
- Fire-resilient design integrated into every component

---

### Ecological Function  
- Mass timber sequesters more carbon than it emits  
- Local forestry ties construction to sustainable landscape management  
- Exposed interiors reduce need for finishing materials, adhesives, and VOCs  
- Roof gardens and courtyards improve stormwater retention and biodiversity

---

### Community Role  
- Developed in collaboration with Swedish housing agencies and private investors  
- Shows that ecological excellence is possible at scale within market frameworks  
- Residents report high satisfaction, increased wellbeing, and spatial quietude  
- A landmark example of design as public trust

---

### Cultural / Symbolic Layer  
- Evokes the Nordic cabinâ€”reimagined for modern cities  
- Restores the narrative of wood as a civic, not just rural, material  
- A poetic response to steel-and-glass anonymity through tactility and climate resonance

---

### Healing Design Principles  
- Daylight-rich units reduce stress and enhance circadian balance  
- Mass timber interiors modulate acoustics and temperature  
- Breathable materials and quietness of form support psychological rest  
- Housing becomes not just shelterâ€”but care infrastructure

---

### Outcomes & Impact  
- Over 240 apartments built in the heart of Stockholm  
- National model for mass timber in residential construction  
- Recognized for carbon-negative footprint and biophilic interiors  
- Strengthened political will for timber-focused building policy

---

### Lessons & Transferability  
- Cities can scale sustainability without aesthetic or emotional compromise  
- Mass timber allows for speed, precision, and poetic intimacy  
- Housing must balance material logic, civic trust, and ecological care
