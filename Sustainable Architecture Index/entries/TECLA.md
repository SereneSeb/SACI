### Summary  
TECLA is a fully 3D printed, biodegradable housing prototype made from local earth, designed as a model for climate-resilient and circular housing.

---

### Design Strategy  
- Double-dome geometry for thermal and structural performance  
- Printed on-site with minimal transportation impact  
- Based entirely on local, renewable materials  
- Optimized for off-grid, low-resource contexts

---

### Ecological Function  
- 100% biodegradable and recyclable  
- Zero-waste construction method  
- Thermal mass of earth regulates interior temperature  
- Uses no concrete, synthetic insulation, or metal

---

### Community Role  
- Designed for replicability and local adaptation  
- Low-cost housing solution for climate-affected regions  
- Proposes a regenerative alternative to global housing markets  
- Can be deployed rapidly in post-disaster or migration contexts

---

### Cultural / Symbolic Layer  
- Reclaims earth as a high-tech and sacred material  
- Merges ancestral building knowledge with digital tools  
- Form echoes traditional dome and hut typologies  
- Architecture as care, not commodity

---

### Healing Design Principles  
- Natural materials create sensory warmth and safety  
- Acoustic dampening and thermal buffering promote calm  
- Off-grid autonomy fosters resilience and self-determination  
- Circularity reflects ecological wholeness

---

### Outcomes & Impact  
- Global recognition as a breakthrough in earth-based 3D printing  
- Inspired research on low-carbon digital construction  
- Validated the feasibility of decentralized, material-conscious housing  
- Bridged design, fabrication, and sustainability discourse

---

### Lessons & Transferability  
- Earth is a viable 3D printing material for housing  
- Circular, zero-waste construction is achievable at scale  
- Regenerative design can be technologically advanced  
- Communities can be empowered to build without global supply chains
