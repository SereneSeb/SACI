### Summary  
A waste-to-energy plant transformed into a public ski slope and park‚ÄîCopenHill reimagines infrastructure as civic playground and ecological statement.

### Design Strategy  
- Converts 440,000 tons of waste annually into clean energy and district heating  
- Rooftop ski slope, hiking trails, caf√©, and climbing wall enable year-round recreation  
- Transparent envelope reveals waste-to-energy process to public view  
- Building acts as landscape‚Äîpermeable, active, multisensory

### Ecological Function  
- Replaces landfill use with high-efficiency energy recovery  
- Significantly reduces carbon emissions from incineration through clean flue technology  
- Provides new green habitat in dense urban fabric  
- Helps normalize and visualize climate-scale material cycles

---

### Community Role  
- Skiers, hikers, tourists, and locals interact with the space daily  
- Schools and environmental groups use the site for learning and tours  
- The plant becomes a stage for civic spectacle and environmental education

### Cultural / Symbolic Layer  
- Waste becomes visibility  
- Machinery becomes theater  
- The civic becomes joyful  
- CopenHill reflects a Danish ethos: transparency, humor, and ecological responsibility

### Healing Design Principles  
- Landscape walkability on infrastructure reduces disconnection  
- Visual access to climate function decreases public anxiety  
- Movement and nature atop machines reframe the city as organism  
- Offers catharsis, curiosity, and ritual movement through ecological contradiction

- Repositions utility buildings as community touchpoints
