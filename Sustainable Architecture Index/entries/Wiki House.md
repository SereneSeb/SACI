### Summary  
WikiHouse is an open-source modular housing system that empowers communities to digitally fabricate and assemble their own sustainable buildings.

---

### Design Strategy  
- CNC-milled modular timber components  
- Parametric design templates shared freely online  
- Local customization based on climate and context  
- Assembly without cranes or toxic adhesives

---

### Ecological Function  
- Material-efficient fabrication with low-waste output  
- Timber construction with carbon-sequestering potential  
- Adaptable to passive solar strategies and site-specific ventilation  
- Encourages reuse and upgrade rather than demolition

---

### Community Role  
- Built by communities in participatory workshops  
- Fosters knowledge transfer and local resilience  
- Often deployed in underserved or transitional communities  
- Challenges centralized production and housing monopolies

---

### Cultural / Symbolic Layer  
- Architecture as open knowledge  
- Technology as civic empowerment  
- Design as a collective language and process  
- Challenges industrialized housing as the only future

---

### Healing Design Principles  
- Breathable interiors with timber and natural linings  
- Empowerment through participatory design and self-build  
- Structures scaled to humans, families, and neighborhoods  
- Encourages relationship to material, place, and community

---

### Outcomes & Impact  
- Used in disaster relief, transitional housing, and urban prototypes  
- Active global network of designers, makers, and facilitators  
- Democratizes sustainable construction knowledge  
- Serves as a civic platform for architectural experimentation
