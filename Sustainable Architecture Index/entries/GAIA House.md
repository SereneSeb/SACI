### Summary  
GAIA House is a 3D printed, biodegradable home constructed from agricultural waste and natural binders, demonstrating a circular, climate-resilient approach to housing.

---

### Design Strategy  
- Material mix of clay soil, rice husks, straw, and lime  
- Layered 3D printing using WASPâ€™s Crane system  
- Passive solar and thermal mass for temperature stability  
- Fully biodegradable and locally reproducible

---

### Ecological Function  
- Entirely compostable if left to decay  
- No high-energy materials or toxic residues  
- Printed directly on-site, reducing emissions  
- Waste-based material use closes agricultural loops

---

### Community Role  
- Demonstrates low-tech, low-cost sustainable housing  
- Can be replicated in farming regions or post-disaster areas  
- Empowers communities to construct without industrial dependence  
- Challenges extractive building cultures

- A poetic return to soil, in form and fate
