### Summary  
Woodside Care Village is a residential dementia care community designed around freedom, familiarity, and safety. It offers neighborhood-style living with therapeutic gardens and inclusive care.

---

### Design Strategy  
- Domestic-scale housing clusters with unique household identities  
- Themed interiors reflect life histories to orient residents  
- Enclosed gardens and walking loops to encourage safe mobility  
- High-street style common areas with recognizable landmarks  

---

### Ecological Function  
- Gardens include edible plants, flower beds, and wildlife-friendly features  
- Outdoor access embedded into each household unit  
- Stormwater management integrated through permeable pathways  
- Daylight use and ventilation reduce environmental stress indoors

---

### Community Role  
- Blends care infrastructure with community interaction  
- Events and performances hosted in shared spaces  
- Residents help prepare meals, tend plants, and engage in daily rituals  
- Relationships emphasized over routines

---

### Cultural / Symbolic Layer  
- Households reflect working-class and regional identities  
- Personal objects displayed in common rooms  
- Storytelling, meal preparation, and sensory engagement embedded in space  
- The built environment affirms the self, not the symptom

---

### Healing Design Principles  
- Multiple sensory modesâ€”light, smell, sound, tactilityâ€”support orientation  
- Familiarity in form fosters safety, calm, and daily confidence  
- Autonomy is protected within safe perimeters  
- Architecture invites relationship, not retreat

---

### Outcomes & Impact  
- Improved mental well-being and reduced medication use  
- Positive feedback from families and residents  
- Inspired future care village models in the UK and Europe  
- Deepened cultural integration within care frameworks

---

### Lessons & Transferability  
- Care design must reflect memory, not just medicine  
- Small-scale homes foster stronger relational patterns  
- Design for neurodivergence requires participatory knowledge  
- Environments can honor lives, not just manage decline
