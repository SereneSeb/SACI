### Summary  
Traditional Ladakhi villages exemplify high-altitude ecological adaptation, using adobe, whitewash, and solar orientation to create passive thermal comfort in extreme climates.

---

### Design Strategy  
- Adobe walls (45â€“60 cm thick) store heat in winter, cool interiors in summer  
- South-facing windows maximize passive solar gain  
- Flat roofs for drying food, stargazing, and seasonal ritual  
- Whitewashed exteriors reflect solar glare and preserve internal coolth

---

### Ecological Function  
- All materials sourced from immediate surroundings  
- Minimal-to-zero carbon footprint over life cycle  
- Organic decay and renewal integrated into seasonal maintenance  
- Climate-resilient without mechanical intervention

---

### Community Role  
- Labor is shared and cyclical, tied to agricultural and spiritual calendars  
- Homes host extended families, prayer rituals, and food preservation  
- Children learn building through practice, not blueprint  
- Social cohesion emerges from shared craft, not centralized planning

---

### Cultural / Symbolic Layer  
- Walls contain sacred niches; lintels carved with protective symbols  
- Orientation linked to sacred topographies and elemental alignment  
- Roof terraces used for offerings, ritual rest, and seasonal festivals  
- Building becomes embodiment of time, belief, and belonging

---

- Insulation creates deep sensory stillness  
- Courtyard and terrace use regulate mood and communal rhythm
