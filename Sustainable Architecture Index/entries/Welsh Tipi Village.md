### Summary  
The Welsh Tipi Village is a low-impact community rooted in seasonal living, ecological simplicity, and symbolic reconnection with land through tipi-based architecture.

---

### Design Strategy  
- Circular tipis arranged around shared hearths  
- No foundations or permanent utilities  
- Compost toilets and rainwater collection  
- Structures rebuilt and renewed seasonally

---

### Ecological Function  
- Minimal ground disturbance  
- Composting and rewilding integrated into daily life  
- Locally sourced timber and canvas  
- Cyclical, off-grid pattern encourages soil regeneration

---

### Community Role  
- Hosts intentional, consensus-based community  
- Supports collective food prep, healing rituals, and ecological learning  
- Site of personal and communal transformation  
- Symbol of land-based resistance and ecological care

---

### Cultural / Symbolic Layer  
- Tipi as archetype of mobile shelter and sacred circle  
- Practices link to solstice gatherings and Earth-based ritual  
- Collective structures echo anti-nuclear and land-rights activism  
- Architecture as a cultural pauseâ€”a refusal of speed and permanence

---

### Healing Design Principles  
- Fire and circularity support social intimacy  
- Silence and dark promote rest and introspection  
- Seasonal rhythm offers ecological and spiritual recalibration  
- Community fosters interdependence and reflection

---

### Outcomes & Impact  
- Sustained over decades as model of low-impact life  
- Inspired other micro-communities and land-based living sites  
- Continues to challenge zoning and development norms  
- Integrates architecture with activism, retreat, and renewal

---

### Lessons & Transferability  
- Sacredness can emerge from simplicity  
- Housing need not always be permanent to be meaningful  
- Low-impact life is architectural, not just lifestyle  
- Seasonal dismantling can be a ritual, not a failure
