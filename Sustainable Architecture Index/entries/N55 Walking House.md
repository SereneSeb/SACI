### Summary  
The Walking House is a mobile, modular housing system that offers off-grid, nomadic living through an autonomous, legged structure.

---

### Design Strategy  
- Modular structure mounted on six robotic legs  
- Powered by solar and wind energy systems  
- Interior includes bed, kitchen, composting toilet, and storage  
- Designed to move slowly while occupied

---

### Ecological Function  
- Entirely off-grid with renewable energy and water reuse  
- Reduces ecological footprint by avoiding permanent land use  
- Demonstrates low-impact nomadic settlement  
- Minimal material use and high reusability

---

### Community Role  
- Imagines new forms of temporary settlement and ecological cohabitation  
- Designed to form walking villages or remain solitary  
- Encourages symbiotic relationships between humans and terrain  
- A speculative model for post-climate migration

---

### Cultural / Symbolic Layer  
- A critique of rootedness, property, and permanence  
- Embodies a hybrid between architecture and organism  
- Symbolizes a new era of planetary wandering and adaptive life  
- Invokes ancestral and future imaginaries of mobility

---

### Healing Design Principles  
- Encourages active engagement with place and movement  
- Disorients routine, offering openness to transformation  
- Living in motion cultivates resilience and reflection  
- Reduces psychological burden of ownership and fixed habitation

---

### Outcomes & Impact  
- Catalyzed global conversations on autonomy and speculative housing  
- Featured in architecture, art, and sustainability discourse  
- Inspires micro-ecological thinking and adaptive housing prototypes  
- Remains a landmark in mobile, conceptual architecture

---

### Lessons & Transferability  
- Architecture can move, adapt, and question territory  
- Autonomy is ecological as well as technical  
- Radical design can still respond to real ecological pressures  
- Temporality is a design variable
