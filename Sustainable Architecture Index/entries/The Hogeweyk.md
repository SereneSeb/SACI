### Summary  
The Hogeweyk is a dementia village designed to simulate normal life through architectural familiarity, spatial routine, and relational care. It reframes care infrastructure through healing design principles and community integration.

---

### Design Strategy  
- 23 domestic-scaled homes arranged along pedestrian streets  
- Public square, theater, grocery store, and green spaces  
- Care teams assigned per house, maintaining resident continuity  
- Street and house types reflect different cultural backgrounds and lifestyles  

---

### Ecological Function  
- Gardens support seasonal engagement and food growing  
- Natural light, fresh air, and quiet zones support health and mood  
- Tactile and olfactory cues from landscape provide navigational markers  
- Aging in place reduces transitions, trauma, and environmental burden  

---

### Community Role  
- Residents participate in daily routines with autonomy  
- Neighborhood design invites volunteers, family visits, and interaction  
- Built to resemble a Dutch village to maintain continuity of cultural memory  
- Reduces institutionalization stigma and fosters relational connection  

---

### Cultural / Symbolic Layer  
- ‚ÄúVillage‚Äù over ‚Äúfacility‚Äù reframes the moral architecture of care  
- Daily rituals as cultural inheritance: baking bread, sweeping, sitting by the garden  
- Redesigns eldercare as shared life‚Äînot deferred death  
- Architecture becomes love translated into form  

---

### Healing Design Principles  
- Familiar settings reduce cognitive load and anxiety  
- Homes enable social rhythm and domestic identity  
- Gardens and open paths invite motion, freedom, and reconnection  
- Environment promotes mental stability without requiring memory

---

### Outcomes & Impact  
- Reduced use of psychotropic medications and restraint  
- Increased resident happiness, orientation, and longevity  
- Internationally recognized as a model for neurodiverse design  
- Influenced care village prototypes in Germany, Canada, and Australia  

---

### Lessons & Transferability  
- Healing architecture requires attention to routine, memory, and multisensory support  
- Design must follow the logic of lived experience, not just medical protocol  
- A small-scale village can carry planetary significance  
- Emotional safety is a design achievement‚Äînot just a clinical one
