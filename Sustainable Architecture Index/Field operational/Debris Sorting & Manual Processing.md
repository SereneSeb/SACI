This operation focuses on the manual sorting and processing of debris in a post-conflict urban area to facilitate safe clearance and material recovery. The objective is to segregate rubble types, identify hazardous materials, and prepare reusable fragments for circular reuse, improving site safety and enabling community reconstruction efforts.

## ‚öôÔ∏è Equipment & Action Plan
### Equipment Used
Personal protective equipment including gloves, dust masks, helmets, and safety goggles. Tools such as shovels, wheelbarrows, sorting bins, and handheld radios for communication were utilized to facilitate manual debris sorting.

### Automated Action Plan (AAP)
The operation followed a structured sequence starting with zone triage to prioritise areas based on debris volume and hazard levels. Manual sorting proceeded in designated sectors, using checklists to identify types of rubble and flag hazardous materials. Routine safety checks and progress logging were integrated to maintain operational flow and ensure data accuracy.

---

## üîç Observations & Findings
The debris primarily consisted of concrete fragments, metal reinforcements, and mixed construction waste, with occasional presence of hazardous materials such as asbestos and unexploded ordnance (UXO). Manual sorting was effective in segregating materials, though progress was occasionally slowed by safety precautions and the need for careful handling of sensitive debris. Constraints included limited lighting in some zones and restricted access due to structural instability.

### Safety & Ethics
- Mandatory use of PPE for all personnel  
- Vigilance for UXO and asbestos with immediate reporting protocols  
- Respectful handling and documentation of any personal effects found  
- Ongoing communication with local community representatives to ensure transparency and cultural sensitivity
